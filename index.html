Market Strategy

The Gap We're Filling

The AEO market is a barbell: cheap SaaS monitoring tools ($30–$300/mo) on one end, full-service agencies starting at $5K–$10K/mo on the other. Almost nothing productized sits between. That gap—$500–$2,500/mo for done-for-you AEO with clear scope—is where Vertia Labs lands.

The biggest differentiator is not tooling (commoditized) but a PR-free citation strategy + cross-lingual entity management + a new, proprietary approach: problem-specific review discovery.

Three Core Differentiators

1. PR-Free Citation Strategy

LLMs cite Reddit, G2, Capterra, YouTube, Wikipedia, and structured first-party content—not press releases. We own these surfaces directly. No journalists, no media relations, no $5K monthly PR line item.

2. Problem-Specific Review Discovery

Proprietary methodology: Identify which specific problems your customers solve, then engineer reviews that explicitly address those problems. Research hypothesis: problem-specific reviews get cited by LLMs 3–4x more frequently than generic testimonials. We validate this with early clients and become first-mover in this emerging moat.

3. Cross-Lingual Entity Management

The only productized offer solving ES↔EN consistency for LatAm brands entering US markets. No other agency explicitly packages bilingual entity parity + hreflang implementation + LatAm SME sourcing for author credibility.

Initial Target Segments

  • LatAm-to-English: YC/Endeavor/Kaszek portfolio companies, revenue-generating, USD spending power. Prefer smaller first step (Blueprint) before retainer commitment.
  • US startups (Seed–Series A): Growing GTM motion, need inbound, AEO-native founders.
  • US SMBs ($3M–$20M): Service categories (SaaS, fintech, professional services), established but zero AEO presence.

Why Problem-Specific Review Discovery Matters

This is not just monitoring which reviews exist. It's a systematic three-phase process:

  • Phase 1 (Month 1–2): Semantic review clustering—identify specific problems your customers solve. Build taxonomy from reviews, sales calls, CRM data.
  • Phase 2 (Month 2–3): Intent-aligned review strategy—create templated outreach asking customers specifically about those problems, not generic feedback.
  • Phase 3 (Month 3+): Citation optimization—measure which problem-framed reviews get cited by LLMs, then double down on high-citation themes.

Result: By month 3, you have documented case studies showing which review themes convert to citations. You own this process before competitors catch on. South America first-mover advantage.

Service Delivery Framework

Problem-Specific Review Discovery: Three-Phase Methodology

This is Your Competitive Moat

The hypothesis that problem-specific reviews get cited 3–4x more often is directional but not yet proven. Here's how you validate and own it:

Month 1–2: Semantic Review Clustering

Build the Taxonomy

Analyze client's existing reviews, sales call transcripts, CRM notes. Identify specific problems customers solve using your product. Example: if your product solves "slow API integration," "compliance setup," and "cost control," those are your three problem clusters. Document patterns.

Month 2–3: Intent-Aligned Review Strategy

Create Templated Outreach

Instead of "please leave us a review," use: "If your biggest challenge was [specific problem], would you share a review on G2 about how [product] solved it?" Template the outreach around each problem cluster. Track response rate + quality difference vs generic requests.

Month 3+: Citation Optimization

Measure and Double Down

Track which problem-framed reviews get cited by LLMs. Measure citation frequency by problem theme. Document wins in case studies. By month 3, you have evidence: "Reviews addressing [problem A] get cited 4.2x more often than generic testimonials."

Validation Path for First 3 Clients

Position them as co-research partners: "We're testing a new hypothesis: problem-specific reviews get cited 3–4x more. You're getting early access to this process. In exchange, we document your results as case studies."

This removes pricing objections, creates urgency, and gives you the data you need to prove—or refine—the hypothesis. By month 6, you have publishable findings.

Monthly Workflow Across All Tiers

Month 1: Diagnostic & Foundation Setting

Goal: Visible momentum. Client sees week-2 movement.

  • Week 1: Deep AEO audit. Crawl site for LLM accessibility, entity inventory, visibility baseline. Schema gap inventory.
  • Week 1–2: First piece of AEO content in production. Awareness-stage or comparison format.
  • Week 2: Core schema markup deployment (Organization, FAQ, Service schema). llms.txt + robots.txt.
  • Week 2–3: For LatAm clients: hreflang audit + implementation. Entity graph baseline.
  • Week 3: Kickoff call recapping findings. Month 1 deliverables already shipped.
  • End of month: Month-1 LLM visibility baseline dashboard. Client sees starting point clearly.

Months 2–3: Compounding & Scale

Goal: Pattern formation. Client sees content cadence, review campaign movement, visibility trending up.

  • Ongoing: Content production on schedule (2–8 pieces/mo depending on tier). Mix of formats.
  • Ongoing: LLM visibility tracking on cadence (monthly, biweekly, or weekly).
  • Month 2: Review campaign launch. Implement problem-specific review taxonomy here. Template outreach around customer problem clusters.
  • Month 2: Answer-first refactor of top pages (Momentum/Citation Engine).
  • Month 3: Entity graph deepening. Author pages, sameAs linking, cross-lingual entity parity (LatAm).
  • Reporting: Monthly (Foundation), biweekly (Momentum), weekly (Citation Engine). Always include: LLM mentions, review velocity, branded search proxy, problem-specific review citation rates.

Months 4–6: Proof & Momentum Building

Goal: Substantive proof. LLM visibility trending, review velocity up, branded search lift visible.

  • Ongoing: All month 2–3 activities continue.
  • Month 5–6: Deeper competitive analysis (Momentum/Citation Engine).
  • Month 5–6: Citation Engine: proprietary data drop launched (quarterly benchmark or survey).
  • Month 6: Problem-specific review hypothesis validation report. Share findings: "Reviews addressing [Problem A] = 4.2x citation rate vs baseline." This becomes your case study positioning.
  • Month 6: For LatAm: ES↔EN entity parity report, hreflang optimization review, bilingual strategy adjustment.
  • Reporting: By month 6, you have 6 months of LLM visibility data + review data + problem taxonomy analysis. This is the narrative for renewal or upsell.

Design System: Glass Morphism & Effects

Glass Cards

Background: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.6–0.8) · Backdrop filter: blur(12–16px) · Border: 1px solid rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.8–0.9)

Glass cards create depth and visual layering. Use sparingly—too much translucency breaks hierarchy. Combine with subtle shadows (0 10px 30px rgba(11, 22, 40, 0.04)) for floating effect.

Shadows

Subtle: 0 10px 30px rgba(11, 22, 40, 0.04) — default cards. Hover: 0 20px 48px rgba(11, 22, 40, 0.08) — on interaction. Accent: 0 10px 24px rgba(124, 58, 237, 0.25) — gradient elements.

Responsive Design

  • Desktop (≥1000px): Full multi-column layouts, generous spacing, large typography.
  • Tablet (768px–999px): 2-column grids, medium typography (h1: 56px).
  • Mobile (≤767px): Single column, stacked components, reduced padding (1rem instead of 2.5rem), optimized h1 (48px), nav links hidden (hamburger on desktop if needed).
