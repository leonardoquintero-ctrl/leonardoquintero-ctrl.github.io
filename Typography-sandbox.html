Font Recommendations by Use Case

The best typographic choice depends entirely on context. Select a use case to explore recommended typefaces and their characteristics.

Sans-Serif for Interfaces

For web apps, dashboards, and navigation, sans-serif fonts are highly recommended. They scale well for small labels and maintain clarity at low resolutions.

  • ✓
    Inter — Designed specifically for screens. Exceptional legibility at small sizes.
  • ✓
    Roboto — Clean, geometric, and functional across all weights.
  • ✓
    Open Sans — Friendly and readable even at 12px on mobile.

Serifs for Extended Reading

For articles, blog posts, and documentation, serif fonts reduce eye fatigue. The "feet" on letterforms guide the eye along the line during long reading sessions.

  • ✓
    Merriweather — Large x-height makes it highly readable on screens.
  • ✓
    Lora — Contemporary, well-balanced, works across device densities.
  • ✓
    Georgia — Web-safe classic. Never fails for body text.

Why Sans-Serif Dominates UI
Geometric letterforms align with the pixel grid of screens. At 12–14px in a navigation label, Inter outperforms Georgia on readability by ~23% in eye-tracking studies.
The Serif Comeback in Long-Form
With high-DPI Retina displays, serifs have regained ground in editorial design. At 16px+ on a 2× display, Merriweather is measurably easier to read over extended sessions.

The Architecture of Information

Good design is as little design as possible. When we apply this principle to web typography, we realize that the primary function of text on a screen is communication. If the user notices the font before they comprehend the message, the typography has failed its primary objective.

Creating Visual Hierarchy

By pairing a highly stylized heading font with a neutral, legible body font, we establish a clear visual hierarchy. This guides the reader's eye naturally from the title, down into the substance of the copy, ensuring a frictionless reading experience across every device.
